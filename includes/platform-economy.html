Risk & platform economy

Risk assessment in the context of the digital platform economy

Risk assessment in the platform economy is bound to be difficult. A significant share of the activities in the platform economy are digital and therefore, hard to imagine/visualise. People often struggle to acknowledge that things they cannot fully visualise can have a massive effect on tangible things like a company’s profit, public health, or the environment.

Difficult, however, does not mean impossible. Three steps can rapidly increase anyone’s ability to undertake risk assessment in (or of) the platform economy.1

Step #1—Identify risks

All risk assessment shares a need for identifying threats/hazards---thing that can go wrong. The platform economy is no different: online activities can go wrong in various manners.

Therefore, a first step is to list things that are difficult to manage in the context of the platform economy.

Comprehensiveness is unlikely, but even if not comprehensive, an initial set of concerns can act as a preliminary list of hazards to improve over time – figure 1 gives an example of one such list (click to enlarge).

Step #2—Traditional risk assessment

Not all concerns in a list of hazards will apply to all types of online actors. A next step is therefore selecting which hazards apply to which actors.

Then comes the hard manual work that no one likes, which is to go one by one and estimate the likelihood of each applicable hazard realising into real-world events, the potential impact of one such event, the combined risk score, and how certain/uncertain you are in your estimates.

Initial estimates will likely be inaccurate, but these can be improved cyclically—as visualised in figure 2 (click to enlarge).

Step #3—Configurational (life cycle) risk assessment

I believe you do not run before you can walk. For me, the previous steps are not optional. You master the basics; then you increase the sophistication of your assessment—never the other way around!

That said, it is possible to move from the bare-knuckles risk assessment in the previous section to a more sophisticated understanding of how risks evolve in the context of the platform economy.

How? Easy!

For all applicable hazards, layout all possible states/scenarios and follow each hazard as a product/service makes its way through the different levels of the platform economy—as visualised in figure 3 (click to enlarge).

Note.Figure 3 offers and example for illustrative purposes and refers to a single hazard. There is a need to do this for each hazard from previous steps.
